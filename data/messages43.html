Bigrams and trigrams are examples of two specific n-grams ( https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/N-gram ), with respectively n=2 and n=3.

For the word "snowflake" these bigrams are created (n=2): sn, no, ow, wf, fl, la, ak, ke. Concatenated, these become "snnoowwffllaakke".

Similarly, for the word "mistletoe" these trigrams are created (n=3): mis, ist, stl, tle, let, eto, toe.
Concatenated these become "misiststltleletetotoe".

If the concatenated word is permuted in a random order, an n-gram-anagram is created.
