I'll try to really simplify this:
Nameserver (NS) gives complete control of DNS (all of the below) to another DNS Server. NS looks like this: ns1.server.com; ns2.server.com

All of the below are DNS Records:

A Name: Address record. It lets the web know where to find your domain's content. It;s simply the IP Address of your server. It looks like 123.534.124.2

CNAME: Also known as alias. It lets you send sub domains of a site elsewhere. You can host all subdomains to the same place as the main site by setting the name to * or *.findrap.xyz (depending on whether your dns manager wants you to use full name)
